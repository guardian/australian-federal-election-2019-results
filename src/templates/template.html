Notes

The 2019 Australian election campaign has been running since May 11, with Scott Morrison leading the Coalition as the incumbents opposed by the Labor party, led by Bill Shorten. Voting (aside from pre-poll voting) opens on May 18, and the first results are expected after 6pm EST.

Results on this page are determined through a combination of the Australian Electoral Commission's data feed and analysis by Guardian Australia.

The policy positions were determined by condensing each of the major party's policies into short position statements for six areas, based on our exisitng policy guide. These positions were sent as a survey to a selection of minor parties and independents running for the lower house. Where no response was received, we have compiled our best guess for the party or candidates policy based on previous public statements.
